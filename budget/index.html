üìä Understanding Student Budgets ‚ñº

Budgeting doesn't have to be boring! Think of it as giving your money a job so you can afford what you actually care about - whether that's nights out, travel, or just not eating instant noodles for the 5th day in a row.

The 50/30/20 Rule: This is your budgeting best friend!

‚Ä¢ 50% on Needs: Rent, bills, groceries, transport - the stuff you literally can't live without.

‚Ä¢ 30% on Wants: Nights out, takeaways, Netflix, that overpriced coffee - basically fun stuff!

‚Ä¢ 20% on Savings: Emergency fund, paying off debt, or saving for something bigger.

Reality Check: On a student budget, you might do 60/30/10 - and that's totally fine! The key is being aware of where your money goes.

Pro Tip: Use the budget calculator in this hub to see how you're doing. You might be surprised!

üéì Student Loans Demystified ‚ñº

Student loans sound scary, but they're actually pretty chill compared to other loans. Here's the real deal:

You Only Pay When You Earn: No income over ¬£27,295/year? You pay ¬£0. Literally nothing.

It's Not Like Other Debt: Missing payments won't wreck your credit score or get you visits from bailiffs. It's automatically taken from your paycheck like tax.

How Much Will You Actually Pay?

‚Ä¢ Earn ¬£30,000? You pay about ¬£20/month (that's 2 Domino's pizzas)

‚Ä¢ Earn ¬£35,000? You pay about ¬£60/month

‚Ä¢ Earn ¬£40,000? You pay about ¬£95/month

The Math: You pay 9% of everything you earn ABOVE ¬£27,295. So if you earn ¬£28,295, you only pay 9% of ¬£1,000 = ¬£90/year = ¬£7.50/month.

It Gets Written Off: After 30 years (Plan 5) or 40 years (Plan 2), whatever you haven't paid disappears. Most people never pay it all back - and that's totally fine!

Bottom Line: Don't stress about student loans. They're basically a "graduate tax" that adjusts to what you can afford.

üí≥ Credit Cards: Smart Use Guide ‚ñº

Credit cards get a bad rep, but used wisely they're actually brilliant. Here's how to use them without falling into the trap:

The GOLDEN RULE: ALWAYS pay the FULL balance EVERY month. Not the minimum payment - the FULL amount. Set up a direct debit for this!

Why Bother With Credit Cards?

‚Ä¢ Build Your Credit Score: You'll need this for future mortgages, car finance, even some rental applications.

‚Ä¢ Section 75 Protection: Buy something ¬£100+ and the seller goes bust? Your credit card company has to refund you.

‚Ä¢ Cashback & Rewards: Free money! Get 0.5%-3% back on purchases. On a ¬£5,000/year spending, that's ¬£25-¬£150 free.

‚Ä¢ Better Fraud Protection: Someone steals your card? The bank's money is at risk, not yours.

How to NOT Mess It Up:

1. Only spend what you already have in your bank account

2. Set up automatic full payment from your current account

3. Never withdraw cash on a credit card (fees are insane!)

4. Never miss a payment (ruins your credit score)

Student Credit Cards: Start with a student card (usually ¬£500-¬£1,000 limit). Use it for Netflix, Spotify, groceries - then pay it off immediately.

üè¶ Building Your Credit Score ‚ñº

Your credit score is like your financial reputation. Future landlords, phone companies, and mortgage lenders all check it. Start building it NOW while you're young!

What Helps Your Credit Score:

‚Ä¢ Being on the Electoral Roll: Register to vote at your uni address (takes 5 minutes online)

‚Ä¢ Paying Bills on Time: Phone contracts, credit cards, even Netflix if it's in your name

‚Ä¢ Using Credit Responsibly: Have a credit card, use it regularly, pay it off in full

‚Ä¢ Staying at the Same Address: Moving every year for uni hurts, but that's life. Just keep your records updated.

What HURTS Your Credit Score:

‚Ä¢ Missing payments (even just the ¬£10 minimum on a credit card)

‚Ä¢ Maxing out credit cards (even if you pay them off)

‚Ä¢ Applying for lots of credit in a short time (looks desperate)

‚Ä¢ Having no credit history at all (banks can't assess you)

‚Ä¢ Payday loans (massive red flag to lenders)

Check Your Score Free: Use ClearScore, Experian, or Credit Karma. Check every few months to make sure there's no mistakes!

üí∞ Emergency Funds: Your Safety Net ‚ñº

Life happens. Laptops break. Phones die. You need to get home urgently. An emergency fund means you're prepared, not panicking.

How Much Do You Need?

‚Ä¢ Minimum: ¬£250-¬£500 (covers most student emergencies)

‚Ä¢ Ideal: ¬£1,000 (covers bigger issues like broken laptop)

‚Ä¢ Gold Standard: 1-2 months of expenses (but this is tough as a student!)

What Counts as an Emergency?

‚úÖ Broken laptop you need for coursework

‚úÖ Urgent trip home for family emergency

‚úÖ Unexpected medical costs

‚úÖ Lost your part-time job and need rent money

‚ùå Festival tickets on sale

‚ùå New trainers you really want

‚Ä¢ Save ¬£20-¬£50/month from your loan or part-time work

‚Ä¢ Put any birthday/Christmas money straight in

‚Ä¢ Keep it in an easy-access savings account (not your current account where you'll spend it!)

üîÆ Future You: Pensions & Long-Term Money ‚ñº

Okay, pensions sound boring AF, but this is genuinely the most important financial advice you'll get. Future You will thank Present You!

Why Care About Pensions NOW?

Because of magic compound interest! Money you save at 22 grows WAY more than money saved at 32.

Real Example:

‚Ä¢ Save ¬£100/month from age 22 to 68 = ¬£450,000 pension pot

‚Ä¢ Save ¬£100/month from age 32 to 68 = ¬£250,000 pension pot

Same monthly amount, but starting 10 years earlier gets you ¬£200,000 MORE!

Workplace Pensions:

When you get a "proper" job, your employer MUST enroll you in a pension. They'll match your contributions (up to a point). This is FREE MONEY - always take it!

‚Ä¢ You pay: 5% of salary (comes out before tax, so feels like 4%)

‚Ä¢ Employer pays: 3% of salary

‚Ä¢ Government adds: Tax relief (basically 1% more)

So you pay 4%, but 9% goes into your pension. That's more than DOUBLE your money!

What If You're Self-Employed?

Open a Personal Pension (Vanguard, PensionBee are good). Even ¬£50/month now will make you rich when you're old.

The One Rule: Never opt out of workplace pensions. Never. Even if money is tight. The employer match is too good to miss!
