Renting Rights

Deposits, landlords & repairs

‚ñº

üëá Tap each card for details

30 days
Deposit protection
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? Your landlord MUST protect your deposit in a government-approved scheme (DPS, MyDeposits, or TDS) within 30 days of receiving it. This is the law!

Why it matters: If they don't, you can take them to court and claim 1-3x your deposit back. They must also give you the scheme details within 30 days.
2 months
Landlord's notice
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? If your landlord wants to evict you (Section 21 no-fault eviction), they must give you at least 2 months' written notice.

Why it matters: They can't just kick you out! They need proper paperwork and a court order. Never let them change locks or intimidate you.
24 hours
Visit notice required
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? Your landlord must give you at least 24 hours' notice before visiting the property (except in emergencies).

Why it matters: It's your home during the tenancy. They can't just show up whenever they want. You have a right to privacy and "quiet enjoyment" of the property.
üö® Critical Info
If your deposit isn't protected properly, you have grounds to sue. Your landlord also CAN'T evict you without following proper legal procedures and getting a court order.

‚úÖ Your Landlord Must:

  • Protect your deposit within 30 days
  • Fix structure, heating, plumbing, electrics
  • Do annual gas safety checks
  • Provide EPC (minimum rating E)
  • Give proper notice before visiting
üí° Pro Tip
Take photos/videos of EVERYTHING on move-in day with timestamps. Document any existing damage. This is your evidence if there's a dispute about your deposit!

What is this? The current cost of a TV licence for 2024/25.

Payment options: You can pay in full, monthly by direct debit, or quarterly. In shared houses, split the cost between housemates!
¬£1,000
Maximum fine
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? If you're caught watching live TV or using BBC iPlayer without a licence, you can be fined up to ¬£1,000.

Why it matters: You could also get a court summons. TV Licensing enforcement can visit, but they have NO right to enter without permission.
‚úÖ You NEED one if you:
  • Watch live TV (any channel)
  • Use BBC iPlayer (even catch-up)
  • Stream live broadcasts
üéâ You DON'T need one if:

  • YouTube (non-live)
  • Catch-up TV (not iPlayer)

üí° Don't Need One?

Consumer Rights

Returns, refunds & warranties

‚ñº

üëá Tap each card for details

30 days
Full refund period
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? If something you bought is faulty, not as described, or doesn't work, you have 30 days to return it for a full refund.

Why it matters: The retailer can't force you to accept a repair or replacement during this period. It's YOUR choice to reject the item!
6 months
Repair/replace period
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? Between 30 days and 6 months, the seller must repair or replace faulty goods. They get ONE attempt to fix it.

After 6 months: You can still claim up to 6 years, but YOU must prove the fault existed when you bought it. Keep evidence!
14 days
Online cooling-off
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? When you buy online, you get 14 days to return items for ANY reason - even if you just changed your mind!

Exceptions: Personalized items, perishables, hygiene products (if opened), digital downloads, and tickets can't be returned.
üí≥ Section 75 Protection
Buy anything ¬£100-¬£30,000 on a credit card? Your card company is jointly liable! If the seller won't refund, claim from your bank.

üìù How to Get Your Refund

  1. Contact retailer (polite but firm)
  2. Quote "Consumer Rights Act 2015"
  3. Put it in writing (email = evidence)
  4. Give 14-day deadline
  5. Escalate to manager if needed
  6. Citizens Advice or small claims court
‚ö†Ô∏è Keep Your Receipts!
Take photos of receipts or save email confirmations. Bank statements can also prove purchase. Create a "Receipts" folder in your email!

Work Rights

Age Hourly Rate
21+ (Living Wage) ¬£11.44
18-20 years ¬£8.60
Under 18 ¬£6.40
Apprentice ¬£6.40
üö® Being Underpaid?
That's ILLEGAL. Report to HMRC immediately. Employers can be fined up to ¬£20,000 per worker!

üëá Tap each card for details

20 min
Break for 6+ hours
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? If you work more than 6 hours, you're entitled to at least a 20-minute uninterrupted break.

Why it matters: Your employer can't ask you to work through breaks. If they do, that break doesn't count and they must give you another one!
5.6 weeks
Paid holiday
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? Legal minimum paid holiday per year (28 days for full-time workers, including bank holidays).

Part-time? Calculate: Hours per week √ó 5.6 = Your annual holiday hours. You MUST be paid for unused holiday when leaving a job!
11 hours
Rest between shifts
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? You're entitled to at least 11 consecutive hours of rest between working days.

Also: You must get at least 24 hours off per week (or 48 hours per fortnight). This protects you from being overworked!

üö´ Your Employer CANNOT:

  • Pay below minimum wage
  • Not pay for mandatory training
  • Make illegal wage deductions
  • Discriminate (age, sex, race, etc.)
  • Deny you breaks
  • Use tips to top up to minimum wage
üí° Tips Belong to YOU!
Employers can't use tips to make up your wages to minimum wage level. Tips are EXTRA on top of your proper wage!

Student Driving

Insurance, parking & fines

‚ñº
üö® Never Drive Uninsured!
Penalties: 6 points + unlimited fine + car seized. "Just 5 minutes" doesn't matter - if you're on a public road, you MUST have insurance!

üí∞ Reduce Insurance Costs

  • Black box insurance - Rewards safe driving
  • Pass Plus course - Gets insurer discounts
  • Named driver - Add experienced parent (if they genuinely drive it sometimes)
  • Pay annually - Avoid monthly interest
  • Lower mileage - Cheaper if rarely used
  • Comparison sites - Compare the Market, MoneySuperMarket, Confused
‚ö†Ô∏è NEVER Lie on Insurance
"Fronting" (claiming parent is main driver when you are) invalidates insurance. If you crash, you won't be covered and could face prosecution!

üëá Tap each card for details

¬£200 + 6pts
Phone while driving
Tap for info ‚ñº
Penalty: ¬£200 fine and 6 points on your licence.

New drivers beware: 6+ points in your first 2 years = licence REVOKED. You'd have to retake both tests!

Drink driving (min.)
Tap for info ‚ñº
Penalty: Minimum 12-month ban, up to ¬£5,000 fine, criminal record, possible prison time.

Long-term impact: Insurance will be massively expensive for at least 5 years. Just don't do it!
6+ points
New driver ban
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? If you get 6 or more points within 2 years of passing your test, your licence is automatically revoked.

Starting over: You'd have to apply for a provisional licence and retake both the theory and practical driving tests!
üÖøÔ∏è University Parking
Most unis require permits (¬£100-¬£300/year) and many ban first-years from having cars on campus. Check your specific uni's policy!

üìã Essential Checks

  • Road Tax: Must pay or declare SORN if off-road
  • Breakdown Cover: Highly recommended (¬£7-¬£15/month) - AA, RAC, or Green Flag

Student Money

Loans, grants & budgeting

‚ñº
‚úÖ Student Loans Aren't Scary
You only repay when earning over ¬£27,295/year. You pay 9% of anything ABOVE that. Earning ¬£30k? That's just ¬£20/month! Loan wiped after 40 years (Plan 5).

üëá Tap each card for details

¬£9,250
Max tuition loan/year
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? Maximum tuition fee loan per year for most courses. This money goes directly to your university - you never see it.

Remember: You don't pay this back until you're earning over ¬£27,295/year, and only at 9% of income above that threshold.
40 years
Loan wiped (Plan 5)
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? For Plan 5 (students starting 2023+), any remaining loan balance is wiped 40 years after graduation.

Plan 2 (2012-2023): Wiped after 30 years. Most people never pay back the full amount!
9%
Repayment rate
Tap for info ‚ñº
What is this? You repay 9% of everything you earn ABOVE ¬£27,295.

Example: Earning ¬£30,000? That's ¬£2,705 above threshold. 9% of ¬£2,705 = ¬£243/year (¬£20/month). Not scary at all!

üéÅ Free Money (Don't Pay Back!)

  • Uni bursaries - ¬£1,000-¬£3,000/year for low-income students
  • NHS bursaries - For healthcare students
  • Disability support - Up to ¬£25,000/year
  • Hardship funds - Apply mid-year if struggling
  • Care leavers - Extra support available
‚ö†Ô∏è Student Overdrafts
0% overdraft (¬£1-3k) is great WHILE studying. But after graduation it becomes 40%+ interest. Plan to pay it off before leaving uni!

üí° Money-Saving Hacks

  • UNiDAYS/Student Beans/Totum - Discounts everywhere
  • 16-25 Railcard - 1/3 off trains (¬£30/year, saves ¬£¬£¬£)
  • Amazon Prime Student - 6 months free, then ¬£4.49/month
  • Shop own-brand - Aldi/Lidl = huge savings
  • Meal prep Sundays - Cheaper than meal deals
  • Library books - Don't buy expensive textbooks!
üì± Council Tax Exemption
Full-time students DON'T pay council tax! Get a certificate from your uni student services and send it to your local council ASAP when you move in.
üö® Money Traps to Avoid
NEVER use payday loans (1,500%+ interest!). Be careful with Klarna/Clearpay - makes overspending too easy. Cancel unused subscriptions!
